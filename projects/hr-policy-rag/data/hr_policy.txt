FALTU COMPANY: GLOBAL HUMAN RESOURCES POLICY MANUALDocument Version: 4.0 (Global Technology Operations)Effective Date: July 22, 2026Jurisdiction: UK Employment Law (Employment Rights Act 1996) & International Remote FrameworksIndustry Sector: Information Technology, Software Engineering & Cloud Services1. Equal Employment Opportunity & Anti-Harassment PolicyFALTU Company is committed to providing a work environment free of discrimination and harassment. In alignment with the UK Equality Act 2010, we strictly prohibit discrimination, victimization, or harassment based on protected characteristics: age, disability, gender reassignment, marriage/civil partnership, pregnancy/maternity, race, religion/belief, sex, and sexual orientation.1.1. Anti-Harassment & BullyingZero-Tolerance: Harassment—verbal, physical, or visual—is strictly prohibited. This includes sexual harassment, cyberbullying, and exclusion.Reporting Mechanism: Employees must report incidents to HR or via the anonymous FALTU Ethics Hotline.Investigation: All claims will be investigated by a designated HR compliance officer within 7 working days. Non-retaliation protection is guaranteed for whistleblowers and complainants.2. Code of Conduct, Ethics & Statutory Compliance2.1. Conflict of InterestEmployees must avoid any financial, business, or personal activity that creates a conflict of interest with FALTU Company. Outside employment, directorships, or freelance contracts require explicit, written approval from the Global HR Director.2.2. UK Whistleblowing & Bribery Act ComplianceAnti-Bribery: In strict compliance with the UK Bribery Act 2010, offering, giving, soliciting, or accepting bribes, facilitation payments, or inappropriate corporate gifts is illegal. Gifts exceeding £50 must be declared in the corporate Gift Register.Whistleblowing: Under the Public Interest Disclosure Act 1998 (PIDA), workers who report wrongdoing (e.g., fraud, health and safety breaches) are protected from detrimental treatment or dismissal.3. Workplace Models: Hybrid & International WFH3.1. UK Headquarters Hybrid ModelThe UK workforce operates under a structural hybrid framework designed to balance collaboration with flexibility.Core Office Days: All UK-based employees are required to work from the UK Headquarters a minimum of 3 days per week. Core collaboration days are designated by department heads.Commuting: Travel to the primary office is considered a regular commute and is not reimbursable.3.2. International Work From Home (WFH) ModelFor employees hired outside the UK, FALTU Company operates an entirely remote, WFH model subject to local operational compliance.Dedicated Workspace: International remote workers must maintain a safe, secure, and ergonomic workspace free from distractions.Core Hours: Remote employees must align their schedules to maintain at least 4 hours of overlapping availability with UK British Summer Time (BST) / Greenwich Mean Time (GMT).4. Intellectual Property (IP) Assignment & Open Source Policy4.1. IP Assignment & Proprietary RightsAutomatic Transfer: All inventions, software code, algorithms, documentation, designs, and architectures developed by employees during their employment—whether during working hours or using company resources—belong exclusively to FALTU Company.Disclosure Obligation: Employees must immediately disclose any developed IP to the Chief Technology Officer (CTO) and sign any documents necessary to perfect the company's legal title globally.4.2. Open Source Software (OSS) ContributionPrior Approval: Employees may not introduce, use, or integrate Open Source Software containing restrictive copyleft licenses (e.g., GPL v3) into FALTU Company proprietary codebases without written approval from the InfoSec and Legal teams.External Contributions: Contributing to external open-source projects using company time, infrastructure, or email addresses is prohibited unless explicitly authorized under a specific company-sponsored initiative.5. Restrictive Covenants & Non-Compete Clauses5.1. Non-Compete RestrictionsTo protect FALTU Company’s proprietary software and market position, employees are bound by post-termination restrictions. For a period of 6 months following their Last Working Day (LWD), employees must not accept employment, consult for, or establish a business that directly competes with the specific technology products or tech services offered by FALTU Company.5.2. Non-Solicitation of Clients and StaffFor a period of 12 months post-employment, departing employees are strictly prohibited from:Poaching Staff: Inducing or attempting to induce any employee or contractor of FALTU Company to leave their position.Poaching Clients: Soliciting, interviewing, or diverting business away from any client or active prospect handled by the employee during the final 12 months of their tenure.6. Information Security (InfoSec) & Acceptable Use Policy6.1. Data Privacy (UK GDPR & International Regulations)In compliance with the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018, processing personal data of clients, users, or employees must be highly secure. Data must never be downloaded onto personal hardware or external cloud drives.6.2. Network & Device ArchitectureVPN Requirements: All WFH and Hybrid employees must connect exclusively via the FALTU Secure VPN.Access Control: Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is mandatory for all access points. Passwords must be a minimum of 14 characters and updated every 60 days.Loss Reporting: Company-issued laptops must never be left unattended in public spaces. Loss or theft of a device must be reported to the InfoSec Incident Team within 1 hour.7. Human Resources (HR) & Performance Operations7.1. Recruitment & OnboardingAll headcount requests require formal budgetary sign-off from Finance. Global remote employees undergo a standardized virtual onboarding program, including identity and background checks through local compliant compliance partners.7.2. Performance Appraisals & Probationary ReviewProbationary Period: All new hires are subject to a 6-month probation period, reviewable monthly by their Line Manager.Bi-Annual Appraisals: Continuous performance evaluations occur twice a year via the FALTU Performance Portal, grading engineering outputs, code quality metrics, and alignment with organizational KPIs.8. Finance, Travel & Expense Management8.1. WFH Stipends & Utility AllowancesUK Staff: Hybrid workers receive no stipend for home utilities as the office remains available.International WFH Staff: Remote workers receive a monthly remote work stipend (adjusted to local currency values) to offset high-speed broadband and electricity costs.8.2. Expense Reimbursement & Global PayrollExpense Logs: Business-related expenses must be logged via the FALTU Expense System within 14 days of purchase. Receipts are mandatory.Global Payroll Processing: Payroll is finalized on the 25th of each month. UK staff are paid via PAYE. International remote workers are paid via local Employer of Record (EOR) partners or compliant global payroll systems.9. Client & Customer Relationship Management9.1. Service Level Agreements (SLAs) & Code QualityCustomer support and engineering operations teams must adhere to a strict 24-hour initial response time for all client software bugs and inquiries. Critical tier-1 production outages require an immediate hotfix response within 2 hours.9.2. Authorized Communication ChannelsFormal corporate messaging must only occur through verified company channels (Corporate Email, Jira, Slack, or Microsoft Teams). Using personal messaging apps (e.g., WhatsApp, Telegram) for client communication or sharing code snippets is strictly prohibited.10. Attendance, Leaves & Shift Management10.1. Working Hours & Shift AllowancesStandard Week: The standard full-time work week consists of 37.5 hours.On-Call & Shifts: Engineering and DevOps teams running 24/7 infrastructure monitoring operate on rotational shifts. Staff on active on-call duty receive a standardized shift allowance calculated in their monthly payroll.10.2. Statutory Leave EntitlementsUK Annual Leave: UK employees receive 28 days of paid annual leave (inclusive of bank holidays). Leave must be requested via the HR portal at least two weeks in advance.International Sick Pay: Remote workers outside the UK receive paid time off and sick pay in strict alignment with the statutory laws of their respective countries of residence.11. Separation, Offboarding & Asset Recovery11.1. Resignation Notice PeriodsDuring Probation: The notice period is 1 week for both parties.Post-Probation: Notice scales to 1 month for core tech engineering staff and 3 months for executive leadership and architecture directors.11.2. Offboarding & Asset RecoveryOn or before the Last Working Day (LWD), all company property (laptops, monitors, testing phones, security tokens, ID badges) must be returned. For international WFH employees, HR will arrange a prepaid courier service. Access to all company code repositories, AWS/Azure environments, and communication platforms will be revoked at exactly 5:00 PM on the LWD.12. Bring Your Own Device (BYOD) PolicyFALTU Company operates on a corporate-issued hardware model. However, under exceptional circumstances where personal devices are approved for work access:MDM Enrolment: The device must run the corporate Mobile Device Management (MDM) software to partition personal data from company data.Rooting/Jailbreaking: Rooted or jailbroken mobile devices are banned from accessing FALTU corporate networks.Remote Wipe: The company retains the right to remotely wipe all company-related data from the personal device if security is compromised or upon employee separation.13. Social Media & Public Representation PolicyEmployees must protect the company's brand identity, software integrity, and corporate reputation online.Public Code Repositories: Employees must never upload code snippets, config files, or API keys belonging to FALTU Company to public GitHub, GitLab, or Pastebin profiles.Speaking Engagements: Any tech talks, conference presentations, or public tech blog posts referencing work done at FALTU Company must receive written authorization from the PR and Technology leadership teams.14. Health, Safety & Ergonomic WellbeingFALTU Company takes its duty of care seriously across both hybrid and WFH setups.Display Screen Equipment (DSE): All UK and international employees must complete an annual DSE assessment online to ensure their monitor setups, seating, and desk heights prevent repetitive strain injuries.Mental Health Support: Employees have 24/7 access to the Employee Assistance Programme (EAP), providing confidential mental health support and professional counseling.15. Training, Professional Development & CertificationsAs a fast-growing tech enterprise, FALTU Company supports continuous engineering and professional excellence.Sponsored Certifications: The company will fund approved cloud architecture (AWS, Azure, GCP), cybersecurity, or management certifications.Clawback Clause: If an employee voluntarily resigns within 12 months of completing a company-funded certification or training course exceeding £1,000, they must reimburse the company for the full cost on a pro-rata basis via their final settlement.16. Whistleblowing & Anti-Retaliation ProtectionEmployees who observe unethical software development practices (e.g., intentional data manipulation, consumer privacy breaches, or compliance fraud) are protected from corporate retaliation.Escalation Path: Reports must be filed directly to the Legal Compliance Department.Anonymity: The identity of the reporter will remain confidential throughout the fact-finding and investigation phase.17. Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) & Tech EquityFALTU Company promotes green computing and sustainable technical infrastructure choices.Green Hosting: Our engineering infrastructure prioritizes carbon-neutral cloud data regions wherever feasible.Volunteer Leave: All employees receive 2 paid days per year to volunteer for non-profit organizations, with a special focus on teaching digital skills or supporting tech equity initiatives.18. Disciplinary & Grievance ProceduresA structured and transparent process governs workplace disputes or performance shortfalls.Verbal & Written Warnings: Minor misconduct or sustained failure to meet technical performance standards results in a documented verbal warning, followed by a formal written warning if uncorrected.Performance Improvement Plan (PIP): Employees failing to meet their core engineering KPIs will be placed on a formal 30-to-60-day PIP. Failure to clear the PIP metrics may lead to termination of employment.19. Workplace Violence & Drugs PolicyFALTU Company maintains an absolute zero-tolerance stance regarding violence, intimidation, or substances in physical or digital workspaces.Prohibited Behavior: Verbal threats, physical intimidation, or online harassment across Slack/Teams will result in immediate suspension pending an investigation.Substance Abuse: Working or appearing for client-facing calls under the influence of illegal drugs or alcohol is grounds for immediate summary dismissal.20. Policy Amendments & Global GovernanceThis policy manual is a living document governed by the Global HR Operations and Legal teams.Annual Reviews: Policies are reviewed annually to update legal compliance frameworks across the UK and active international EOR operational areas.Employee Acknowledgment: All employees must digitally sign an acknowledgment form via the FALTU HR portal within 5 working days of receiving this policy handbook or any subsequent revisions.
